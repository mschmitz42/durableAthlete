The wake-up call

Hey Athletes,

Yesterday, we broke down the two beliefs that keep triathletes stuck: pushing through pain or stopping completely.

Today, we’re taking it a step further. In today’s video, I break down the 4 core skills—mobility, technique, strength, and endurance—and how they work together to keep you training strong.

For me, this lesson came the hard way. My wake-up call? An IT band strain that benched me—again. I’d been piling on miles, skipping the “boring” stuff like mobility and technique, and my body finally said enough. During that downtime, I dug in—books, videos, research—anything I could find to figure out why I kept breaking down. Here’s what I learned:

Swim: My form was a mess. Fighting the water every lap—legs sinking, shoulder screaming like it had an ice pick in it. Once I worked on shoulder mobility and basic swim drills, I stopped fighting the water and started gliding like I belonged there.

Bike: My hips were locked from riding and sitting all day. My pedal stroke was choppy, always in the wrong gear, burning out my quads. Mobility work and pedal stroke drills smoothed it out. Suddenly, riding farther wasn’t an issue and I was climbing like a homesick angel.

Run: Plantar fasciitis and I were well-acquainted. Relearning run mechanics and mobilizing my calves and feet was a game changer. Running stopped feeling like something I had to survive—and I actually started to enjoy it.

That’s when it clicked: the basics aren’t just for beginners, and they’re definitely not a waste of time—they’re essential. When you fully engage and work to master them, they’re not boring at all. They’re actually pretty fun. Master the basics, and you unlock your true potential.

Here’s the short version:

  • Mobility: Free up stiff joints. Tight hips, shoulders, and ankles? They’re slowing you down.
  • Technique:Move efficiently. Think smoother run form, better pedal strokes, and easier swimming.
  • Strength:Build stability that protects you.
  • Endurance:Pile this on once the first three are dialed in.

These aren’t optional. They’re the foundation of staying injury-free and finally training the way you’ve always wanted.

Today’s Mobility and Technique Challenge:

Today’s drills might look simple, but don’t underestimate them. Hips-to-heels mobility frees up your ankles, knees and hips so running feels easier. The swim cadence test shows you how to use the water as leverage instead of windmilling your arms like an inflatable car-lot dancer.

  • Daily Mobility: Hips to Heels
  • Technique Drill: Swim Cadence Assessment

Let’s see what you’ve got: post a clip or your takeaways from today’s mobility and swim drill in the Durability Triathlete Facebook group.

Tomorrow, we’ll tackle the next big objection: “I don’t have time.” I’ll show you exactly how to fit this in—even with a packed schedule—so you can keep training without breaking down.

Start where you are. Use what you have. Do what you can.
