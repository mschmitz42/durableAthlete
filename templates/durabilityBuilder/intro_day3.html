The wake-up call

Do you remember the wake-up call when you realized your training approach wasn’t working? I do, and it transformed my entire approach to triathlon.

It happened when I was sidelined by an IT band strain—yet another frustratingly familiar injury. I had been pushing my limits while neglecting the "little things" like mobility and technique. My body was breaking down, my knee hurt like hell, and I knew something had to change.

I took a deep dive into why I kept getting injured and discovered that becoming durable wasn’t about grinding harder; it was about working smarter. Since I thought I couldn’t work out during my downtime, I hit the books—lots of them! I also scoured the internet. Holy crap, there is so much misleading information out there! Anywho…

As I focused on improving my mobility and technique, I finally started to see real progress. I began with swimming, where I struggled to maintain proper form. Let’s be real—I sucked. My breathing was off, my legs sank, and my shoulder felt like it had an ice pick lodged in it. Every stroke felt like a fight against the water. I realized I needed to prioritize my form and technique if I wanted to improve.

When I dedicated time to mobilizing my shoulders and focusing on basic swim drills while relaxing, everything changed. I became more efficient with each stroke. This wasn’t just about correcting weaknesses; it was about finding balance.

Next, I turned my attention to cycling. I had it all wrong, struggling to maintain a smooth pedal stroke, always in the wrong gear and burning out my quads. My hips were incredibly tight from always being in flexion (typical for cyclists). By focusing on each quadrant of my pedal stroke, getting a proper bike fit, and mobilizing my hips, I began to communicate better with my bike. Before I knew it, I was climbing hills like a homesick angel.

And how about that run? This was a big one for me! Let’s just say plantar fasciitis and I are well acquainted. Relearning how to run and mobilize everything below the knee was a total game changer. It took a while, but as my feet and calves finally loosened up, I found joy in running, which made me want to do it more.

Finally, efficient movement allowed me to focus on building endurance. Training without pain enabled me to sustain longer sessions and actually enjoy them.

This journey taught me that the basics aren’t boring; they're essential for unlocking your full potential as a triathlete. The four core elements of durability—mobility, technique, strength, and endurance—are interconnected. In triathlon, just like in life, you’re only as strong as your weakest link. Each skill you master reveals new challenges and opportunities for growth.

In the past, injuries would sideline me, but by prioritizing mobility, technique, strength, and endurance, I finally saw real progress. My body stopped breaking down, and my performance soared.

That’s what my Durability Builder Program is all about: sustainable progress and long-term performance gains. It’s about working smarter, not harder. So let’s embrace the often-neglected and oh-so-crucial elements of mobility and technique together and transform you into a durable triathlete!
