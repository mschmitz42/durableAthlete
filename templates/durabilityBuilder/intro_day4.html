The mileage myth that’s slowing you down

Hey Athletes,

Today’s lesson tackles one of the biggest myths in triathlon: “Miles matter most. I don’t have time for warm-ups, cool-downs, mobility, or drills.”

I used to think this way too. Log more miles, skip the "extras," and magically get faster, right? Nope. That mindset cost me time, progress, and landed me in the injury cycle more than once.

Watch today’s video where I explain why focusing only on miles is holding you back—and what to do instead.

Here’s the shift that changed everything:

  1. Train to prevent the strain. Short, focused warm-ups, drills, and mobility keep you moving well and make you faster.
  2. Treat the strain while you train.If something hurts, you don’t stop everything—you pivot, mobilize, and focus on what you can do. Triathlon gives you options.

Here’s how to make it practical:

  • Warm up and cool down: Even 5 minutes of walking or easy spinning before and after workouts reduces injury risk.
  • Intervals over junk miles: Your body adapts to quality, not just quantity. Focused sessions build speed and form faster than slogging.
  • 3DME Mobility: Just 8–12 minutes a day keeps you loose and durable. Knock it out all at once or split it into 3 short sessions (yes, even while watching TV).

These aren’t “extras.” They’re the difference between steady progress and another forced break from training. You don’t need more hours in the day—you just need a smarter strategy. That’s what we’ll build together.

Today’s Mobility and Technique Challenge:

This one is sneaky. Hanging unlocks your shoulders so swimming feels lighter and posture comes naturally. The barefoot run assessment sharpens your stride so every step is quick, light, and efficient.

  • Daily Mobility: Hang: 2 min chin, 2 min pull
  • Technique Drill: Barefoot run Assessment

Don’t ghost it—drop your barefoot run form or hang time in the Facebook group for some feedback.

Tomorrow, it’s go time. In the workshop, I’ll walk you through building your 6-week durability plan so you can finally break the injury cycle and train consistently.

Start where you are. Use what you have. Do what you can.
