Your comeback story starts now
Fear doesn’t define you—your next step does

Hey Athletes,

As we dive into Day 13 of our Durability Builder, I want to talk about a hurdle many athletes face: the fear of not succeeding.

This fear can be paralyzing, especially when you’re pushing your limits or striving to make a comeback. Maybe you’ve thought:

  • “What if I’m not cut out for this?”
  • “What if I can’t make it back?”

These thoughts are completely normal—we all have them. But here’s the truth: fear doesn’t define you—your response does.

5 Ways to Tackle the Fear of Not Succeeding

  1. Shift Your Mindset: Focus on the journey and the progress you’re making, not just the end goal. Every small step forward is a victory.
  2. Set Realistic Goals: Huge, unmanageable goals can lead to burnout and injury. I’ve coached countless athletes to success by breaking their goals into smart, achievable steps.
  3. Embrace the Process: Setbacks are part of any training journey. They’re not failures—they’re opportunities to learn and grow.
  4. Believe You Can: I didn’t believe I could complete a 140.6 Ironman until I saw friends who’d done it and thought, “If they can, why not me?” That belief was the spark I needed to commit, and the next year, I crossed my first Ironman finish line.
  5. Seek Support: Connect with your teammates or a coach. Sharing your fears lightens the load and brings perspective.

Paula Newby-Fraser, the winningest woman in Ironman history, said it best: “The longest distance in an Ironman is the one between your ears.”

Success isn’t just physical; it’s mental. It’s about conquering fear to unleash your potential.
