The fear that stops most athletes (and how to beat it)

Hey Athletes,

Welcome to Day 13 of the Durability Kickstart—and this one's a biggie.

We’re talking about one of the heaviest hitters that derails progress:
“What if I’m not strong enough to make it back?”

Fear of failure, fear of falling short, fear of making things worse—it’s real.
But it doesn’t get the final say. You do.

I’ve coached hundreds of athletes back from injury, burnout, or flat-out frustration. They didn’t do it with willpower alone—they had a clear structure, support, and belief.

Like Madeline—after knee surgery, she doubted she'd race again. Now? She’s not just finishing Ironmans… she qualified for the freakin' Kona World Championships.

Or Cody. Wrecked knee, two kids, high-stress job. After a brutal first year in triathlon, he was ready to give it up. But with the right plan? He shaved 46 minutes off his Ironman time and stayed injury-free.

This isn’t magic. It’s repeatable. And you’ve already started.

If you’ve made it this far, you’ve done more than just follow a few drills—you’ve proven that you’re serious about change. That deserves some recognition.

Watch Day 13’s video where I share stories of athletes who turned setbacks into triumphs.

This neat trick relieves tension so running feels lighter and more fluid. The Push Stroke & Glide drill teaches you how to move with the water, not fight against it—hello faster splits.
