How to Get Results Even When You're Busy

Ever feel like you’re juggling everything—work, family, training, recovery—and still not seeing the results you want? When you’re balancing life and trying to get back in race shape, it can feel like there’s just not enough time.

But here’s the truth: it doesn’t have to be this hard. You don’t need to check every box or follow a rigid plan to see real progress. You just need to know what to focus on next.

The Problem:
I hear folks say things like, “I’m too busy to keep up with a training plan.”

I get it—life gets busy, and a complicated training plan can feel like just one more thing to squeeze in. But here’s the key: It’s not about cramming everything in all the time. It’s about prioritizing and blending what matters most.

The Solution:
This is where built-in flexibility really shines. The beauty of the 6 Week Durability Builder and the Fully Customized Training Program is that they adapt to your schedule, not the other way around. They’re designed to be efficient and manageable, helping you build durability—the kind of strength that supports both your training and your everyday life.

You don’t have to do everything all the time. Instead, focus on the consistent actions that you can fit into your schedule. It’s the consistency that leads to progress over time.

Here’s what you can do today:

  • Prioritize what matters now: Focus on mobility and technique. These are the building blocks that will keep you injury-free and ready to push harder when you’re able.
  • Blend your training with life: Take a walk with the family, work out with friends, fit in some 3DME during work breaks, or stretch while watching TV. It doesn’t have to be perfect—just consistent.
  • Commit to consistency, not perfection: A few minutes of mobility work throughout the day is more effective than cramming everything into one session.

If you’re still not sure how to make this work with your schedule, let’s set up a 15-minute consultation. I’ll help you figure out how to set the right goals for the time you have so you can keep progressing without feeling overwhelmed.

You deserve to feel better, go faster, and stay out of pain—without the extra struggle.
