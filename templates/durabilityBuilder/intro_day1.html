Day 1: I’m fed up with Strain & Injury…
Ready to Train Smarter, Not Harder? Let’s Start Day 1 of the Durability Builder!

Hey there, Tim Sorensen here!

I know firsthand how frustrating training can be. When I started competing in triathlons at 31, I was overwhelmed by all the conflicting advice. Like many, I thought the answer was to swim hard, bike hard, and run hard—a lot! But all that effort only led to strains, injuries, and falling short of the gains I'd hoped for.

I'd look at the pros and wonder, "If I'm putting in the work, why don't I see those results?" It honestly pissed me off. So, I dug deeper. Years of study, trial, error, and more trial taught me the real key wasn't just doing more; it was focusing on mobility, technique, strength, and injury prevention—and getting the balance right. That's when things finally clicked.

You’ve probably been bombarded with advice: “Swim more, bike more, lift weights, go slower, go faster…” It’s like trying to drink from a fire hose. I get it—it’s overwhelming. So, where do you start? When do you start?

First, start where you are, use what you have, and do what you can. And when? Right now!

Here’s the truth: waiting for that elusive “perfect moment” to train without pain? It’s a myth. The best time to start is now.

Once I shifted my focus to addressing weak points rather than piling on more training, I saw a real change in performance. It wasn’t about training harder; it was about training smarter.

Now, a quick word about pain: not all pain is bad. Good pain—the satisfying burn after a solid workout—means your body’s adapting and getting stronger. Bad pain, though, is the sharp, persistent kind that signals something’s wrong. Learning to spot the difference is crucial.

For over 25 years, I’ve refined this approach, not just for myself but for countless triathletes. They’ve transformed from injury-prone to durable, confident competitors. You don’t need to be sidelined by overuse injuries or self-doubt. Let’s tackle what’s holding you back so you can train and race with confidence.
