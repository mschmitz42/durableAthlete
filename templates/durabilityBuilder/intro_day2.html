The 2 beliefs that have wasted years of progress in triathlon…

Ever feel like no matter how many miles you slog through or how many yards you swim, you just don’t see the progress you were hoping for?

That was me 20 years ago…

Stuck, frustrated, and a little embarrassed by my triathlon performance.

Back then, I was convinced the secret to improvement was simply to push through the pain, believing that discomfort was some kind of twisted rite of passage. I thought if I just kept ignoring those nagging aches, my endurance and speed would magically crank up a notch. Spoiler alert: it didn’t. It was a vicious cycle of burnout—painful, exhausting, and downright soul-crushing.

Every day, I’d lace up my shoes with grand dreams, only to find my body feeling worse than a hangover after a night out. I was waking up feeling like “the morning after” and I hadn’t gone out the night before. The pain cranked up, my pace dropped down, and my confidence went with it.

The self-talk grew louder, saying, "You’re just not tough enough."

Side Bar: Let’s clarify good pain and bad pain. At first, I thought all pain was bad, but then I realized that some of it was actually good—just my body adapting and getting stronger. Unfortunately, I mistook the bad pain—those sharp twinges and throbbing reminders of injury—for just another part of the process. Outwardly, I was battling one injury after another. But the real kicker? I believed the only way to heal was to completely hit the brakes. I thought I’d lose all my hard-earned fitness in the process, which left me trapped in a maddening loop of frustration.

Then, out of nowhere, I stumbled upon a new path—one that flipped everything on its head. It wasn’t about pushing through the pain or becoming a couch potato...

I discovered the game-changing magic of structured technique, mobility, and strength training. This wasn’t just another half-baked training tweak; it was a blueprint for building real durability and optimizing my movement.

This fresh approach didn’t just revamp my training; it catapulted me toward becoming the triathlete I always wanted to be. And guess what? It actually worked…

Not only did my performance finally improve, but my body stopped fighting me, and my confidence skyrocketed.

The outdated beliefs that I had to either push through pain or completely stop to heal? Shattered.

All I needed was a system focused on improving how I moved, and suddenly, everything else fell into place.

So, if you’re feeling trapped in your training, wondering if you’re destined to either suffer or lose your fitness, take a cue from my story…

Durability and mobility training transformed my approach to triathlon, and it might just do the same for you. Today’s exercises, the Couch Stretch, and the bike cadence drill are a really good first. These two activities will give you some of the highest return on time invested making everything else just work better.
